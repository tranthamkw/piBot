<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Function and Purpose</strong></p>
<p>I am a simple platform upon which electrical, mechanical, and software engineering can be taught and explored.&nbsp; The key concepts which may be explored are</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><em>Electrical engineering</em>:</p>
<p>Battery charging management,</p>
<p>RS485 serial bus communication,</p>
<p>Pulse-width-modulation motor speed control,</p>
<p>Servo motor position control, and</p>
<p>Digital display devices.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><em>Mechanical engineering</em>:</p>
<p>strength of materials,</p>
<p>robot locomotion and steering strategies</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><em>Software engineering</em>:</p>
<p>embedded microprocessor control systems (<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Embedded_system">https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Embedded_system</a>),</p>
<p>stereoscopic computer vision,</p>
<p>Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) &ndash;(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simultaneous_localization_and_mapping),</p>
<p>HTML and PHP coding.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><strong>Hardware design</strong></p>
<table>
    <tbody>
        <tr>
            <td width="185"></td>
            <td width="438">
                <p>The central computer is a Raspberry Pi Model XX running Debian/Raspbian operating system. The computer connects to a variety of peripheral devices through a proprietary RS485 serial bus, which allow the robot to interact with the physical world.&nbsp; The peripheral devices are called &ldquo;guests&rdquo; and the Raspberry Pi is the &ldquo;Host&rdquo;.&nbsp; Communication is managed through an addressable serial bus.</p>
